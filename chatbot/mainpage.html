History of Ayurveda

  • Ayurveda, the "Science of Life," originated in India over 5,000 years ago. It is a holistic approach to health that focuses on balancing the body, mind, and spirit. The principles of Ayurveda are based on the belief that health and wellness depend on a delicate balance between the three fundamental bodily doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha.
  • The roots of Ayurveda can be traced back to ancient texts, known as the Vedas, which are some of the oldest scriptures in the world. The knowledge was passed down through generations by sages and practitioners who used a combination of herbal medicine, diet, and lifestyle modifications to maintain health and treat diseases.
  • Today, Ayurveda is recognized globally for its natural and preventive approach to health. It emphasizes personalized treatment plans and the use of natural herbs, minerals, and therapeutic methods, making it a valuable complement to modern medicine.
